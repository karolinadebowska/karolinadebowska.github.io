Architecture

Not everyone knows, but the Students' Union consists of three buildings: The original Graves Building, built in the 1930's, University House and Link Building constructed from 1959-1962.

The Graves Building was donated to the students of the university as a personal gift by J G Graves, a local mail-order businessman and city benefactor. It was designed by the Professor of Architecture, Stephen Welsh. The new building was opened in 1936. The interesting fact is that it was designed to separate men from women, but this didn’t last long.

The Interval Cafe Bar has been there since 2006.

The University of Sheffield then built on two more buildings from 1959-1962. These were the University House and the Link Building.

The new Union building was opened in 1996 and designed by Mott Architecture.

The other building was designed to merge them both.

These two building projects were organised by the University and have been extended several times since 1993 due to a significant increase in a number of students. The lower floors of University House were altered and the Link Building was extended.

The university’s latest project was to refurbish the University House, e.g. by impressive new main entrance. It was completed with £5 million from HEFCE in 2010.

Mathers, H.,(2007). Standing up for students. Sheffield: The University of Sheffield Union of Students.
